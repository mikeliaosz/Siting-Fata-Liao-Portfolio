About

Statement

Sitong (FATA) Liao is a fashion and fiber designer working between China and Chicago. She is currently completing a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Fashion Design and Fiber Materials at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago.

Her practice explores memory, culture, and emotional connection through garments and crafted surfaces, shaped by the experience of living across different cultural contexts. Rather than positioning herself primarily as an independent artist, Liao approaches design as a form of narrative service to industry, helping brands articulate identity through material, form, and storytelling.

Working across draping, structural development, embroidery, fabric manipulation, and illustration, she treats garments as both architecture and archive. Each piece becomes a vessel for stories in which personal memory, literature, and cultural symbolism intersect with contemporary silhouettes. By observing her own experiences from a reflective distance, Liao allows narrative and material process to unfold together within the act of making.

Her collection A Tranquil Night (静夜思) draws from lunar imagery and homesickness, translating the quiet intimacy of longing into tactile form. Inspired by the shared act of looking at the same moon from distant places, the work reflects on separation as a subtle form of connection.

Color plays a central role in her practice. Influenced by the vitality of her hometown, Liao often works with vivid, playful palettes that soften cultural distance and invite resonance across audiences. Through color, texture, and pattern, elements of Chinese cultural memory travel within the garment, reaching viewers who may not share the same background but can still feel its emotional warmth.

Through her work, Liao seeks to create garments that communicate quietly yet deeply—designs that carry stories, invite empathy, and allow cultures to meet through feeling rather than explanation.
